# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Specialty-coffee drinkers and neighborhood regulars in North Nazimabad, Karachi, plus curious newcomers discovering the shop. Visitors arrive on phones (often over mobile data) and on laptops. Many are looking for a place to slow down, put the phone away, and actually talk to the person across the table. The site is also the first impression for anyone deciding whether to make the trip.

## Product Purpose

An immersive editorial website for Mazi Specialty Coffee, a nostalgia-inspired café that asks people to disconnect from technology and reconnect with conversation. The site is not a menu board; it is a cinematic, photographic experience that lets visitors feel the atmosphere before they walk in. Success means a visitor closes the tab with a clear sense of the place's warmth, craft, and character, and knows how to find and visit it.

## Brand Personality

Warm, editorial, cinematic. Three words: nostalgic, handcrafted, inviting. The emotional goal is to make people feel the room, not just read about it.

## Anti-references

Typical coffee-shop templates (latte-art hero with a coupon bar, three-column "why us" cards, stocky warm-beige + brass palettes, generic "Elevate your morning" copy). Overly commercial, list-first layouts. Anything that reads as a SaaS landing page wearing a coffee costume.

## Design Principles

- Show, do not tell. Photography and typography carry the story; copy is warm and specific, never filler.
- Honor the existing visual language. The Spasex-derived system (pure-black canvas, white uppercase industrial display type, full-bleed photography, ghost pill CTAs, hairlines) is the source of truth. Extend it; do not replace it.
- Motion serves storytelling. Smooth scroll, calm reveals, and ambient detail support the narrative and always respect reduced-motion.
- Restraint is the craft. One CTA per band, generous negative space, no accent colors beyond black, white, and the photograph itself.
- Built for the room it represents. Mobile-first, accessible, fast, and easy to update by the owner.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Semantic HTML, logical heading hierarchy, visible focus states, descriptive alt text, and ARIA only where it adds meaning. Full support for `prefers-reduced-motion`: every animation degrades to a calm crossfade or instant state. Touch targets meet AAA sizing. High-contrast white-on-black type throughout.
